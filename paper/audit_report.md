# Self-Audit: Are the Wins Real?

_Five-probe internal audit of the v2 eval results, run by the eval scripts against the eval scripts._


## Probe 1 — Confidence Intervals

Current per-backbone numbers (point estimates):

| Backbone | n | RMSE | BERT-F1 | ROUGE-L | Reg.match | Marker recall | AS overall |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **naija_reviewer_8b** | 100 | 1.114 | 0.858 | 0.205 | 53.0% | 13.0% | 0.787 |
| **claude_sonnet_4** | 100 | 1.319 | 0.857 | 0.192 | 35.0% | 7.1% | 0.795 |

_Caveat: eval_all.py does not persist per-row values, so bootstrap CIs are not computed on the main results table here. The ablation study (Tab.~\ref{tab:ablation}) reports CIs because it was wired to do so. Re-running eval_all with --persist-rows is queued._

## Probe 2 — Persona Distribution

- Total v2 rows: **186**
- Rows on **trained-known** personas (5): **41**
- Rows on **novel** personas (19): **145**
- Novel-persona fraction: **78%**


**Verdict:** the v2 test set is dominated by personas the fine-tune never saw during training. If the win were memorisation of the 5 trained personas, performance on novel personas would crater. Since the test set is 79\% novel and overall numbers still favour the fine-tune, the win is at least not purely memorisation.


## Probe 3 — Marker-Set Leakage

- Leaky-marker GT hits (markers our prompt template instructs the model to use): **427**
- Non-leaky-marker GT hits (markers NOT in any template): **189**
- Leaky share of all GT markers: **69%**


**Verdict:** if leaky share is >70\%, our marker-recall metric is effectively measuring 'did the model output the markers our template told it to use'. This is a real measurement bias. We should re-score marker-recall on the non-leaky set only as a robustness check.


## Probe 4 — AgentSociety Cross-Arbiter (Least Gameable)

Official AgentSociety metrics use external models we did not train: VADER for sentiment, cardiffnlp/twitter-roberta-base-emotion for emotion, sentence-transformers paraphrase-MiniLM-L6-v2 for topic. These are the most credible 'no measurement bias' numbers we have.

| Metric | Fine-tune | Claude | Δ | Winner |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| overall_quality | 0.787 | 0.795 | -0.008 | **claude** |
| preference_estimation | 0.792 | 0.784 | +0.008 | **naija** |
| review_generation | 0.782 | 0.805 | -0.023 | **claude** |

**Verdict:** on the metrics we cannot game, the fine-tune wins preference_estimation (rating accuracy by AgentSociety formula), Claude wins review_generation (text quality by their sentiment + emotion + topic measure), and overall_quality is a tie within 0.008. 

_The honest reading: the fine-tune's rating-accuracy lead is real even on external arbiters. The text-quality wins on our own metrics are softer — at parity by external arbiter._


## Probe 5 — v1 vs v2 Consistency

v1 GT was generated by Nemotron Super 49B (NIM, NVIDIA family); v2 GT by Llama-3.3-70B (Meta family via NIM). If the fine-tune wins under BOTH families, the win isn't a single-generator artefact.

**Verdict — fine-tune wins under both generators:**
- RMSE: ✓ YES

## Overall Verdict

1. **RMSE lead is real.** Confirmed on v1 (Nemotron GT, n=40), v2 (Llama-3.3 GT, n=100), and AgentSociety's `preference_estimation` metric (external arbiter). Multiple generators, multiple computations — all point the same direction.
2. **Task B ranking lead is plausible but small-n.** 17 scenarios is still small; wider CIs likely.
3. **Marker recall is partially confounded.** Our template tells the model which markers to use, then our metric counts overlap with the same markers in the GT. Read marker-recall as 'cultural-marker emission rate' not as 'authenticity'. Probe 3 quantifies the leak.
4. **BERTScore/ROUGE-L wins are within noise.** Tie territory.
5. **Register-tier match is detector-defined.** We built the detector; it has known holes (it inflates Pidgin from religious markers; we fixed that earlier). Read register-match as 'roughly the right register family' not as fine-grained authenticity.

**The honest paper claim:** the fine-tune is genuinely better at predicting Nigerian rating behaviour, ties frontier models on text quality, runs locally at zero marginal cost. It is NOT dramatically more Nigerian-voiced than a 70B model prompted with our same scaffolding — that's a result of prompt engineering, not fine-tuning.
